About me

A brief introduction on my history

Ryan Zurrin

Professional Father & Full Time Student in the Computer Sciences

I'm Berkshire County born and raised, from the wonderful City of Pittsfield Massachusetts where I currently reside and go to school at Berkshire Community College. I will be getting my associates degree at BCC and will then be moving on to get a bachelors, and maybe even a masters degree. I really love computers and electronics, and just learning about how things work and function. My curiousity has carried me all over and has granted me the opportunity to try out many things before finally setteling with the computer sciences. I originally started as a fine arts major, mostly because I had previous experience painting and drawing already so it just made sense. But then I got a position as a computer lab assisstant which rekindled a burning ember that lay dormant within me. In my youth, I gamed all the time and even built my fisrt computer system at 15 (when 32mb of Ram was considered VERY fast). I just loved exploring all the files and system configs and setting that made sofware work, it fascinated me then and still does to this day.

So I made the switch from art to computer science where I hope to some day become a programmer and software engineer. I am planning on using the skills I obtain to help others build up their own ideas and dreams and I really feel using technology as a medium is the best way to reach the most people and the possibilities are truly endless, especially once your able to fuse old ideas with the new technologies. I, like most come from the ground up. I was built on the sweat and tears of my labor and it has carried me here, to school. Where I am learning to do things like this, building a web page, and am learning most importantly that to make the world a better place it takes a true team effort. No one person has the answer but as a team working together the possible solutions become almost endless, a fusing of minds and ideas all built up around us and antoher whole world folded up in my laptop. It is this folded up dimension I am so hungry to learn about. I am looking forward to learning about all there is to learn about designing, debugging, and building fast and efficient software and I’d be lying if I didn’t say I think I may have gotten myself in a little over my head with all this, but I am up to a good challenge and I am ready to give it my all and to do the hard work towards building up a good solid foundation for which I hope can frame the rest of my life around.
